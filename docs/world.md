# World

Setting, tone, and story are deliberately undecided beyond what's fixed below. tro.gg leads with functionality; canon gets figured out later — and much of it is written by the tribe as the world is built and played, not pre-written here.

Three things are fixed:

- **Troggs** are the player characters. Lowercase in prose; `player` in code and schema.
- **The dark** is what troggs push back against. It has no dialogue and no motive beyond hunger for the light; what lives in it — the dark creatures — are threats, not characters.
- **The spark.** Every trogg carries a spark of the tribe's one fire. While its player is here, the spark burns bright: the trogg dares, fights, and pushes the frontline outward. While its player is away, the spark dims to an ember: the trogg drifts on instinct, gathering for the tribe until the ember runs out and it settles, dormant, by whatever hearth is nearest. This is canon, not just mechanics — see [gdd.md](gdd.md) ("The fire and the dark") for the binding rules it implies.

Everything else — zone names, items, monsters, landmarks, story, and art direction — is open. The Hearth (the tribe's hub, built around the First Fire) and the Warren (the birth-cave onboarding, see gdd.md) are the two named fixed points; what lies beyond the frontline, what the dark creatures are, and what the tribe eventually calls the places it wins back are all unwritten.
